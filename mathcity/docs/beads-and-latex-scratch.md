## Original scope statement

Governs how LaTeX work enters and moves through the bead system: what LaTeX
work is a bead, how a LaTeX bead's lifecycle stage is expressed, what the
quality gates prove (and pointedly do not prove), how documents are atomized
into beads, how LaTeX couples to LMFDB records and Magma computations, when
section merges are legal, how compile failures become MREs, and which bead
shapes are anti-patterns. Every rule has an ID and a pass/fail criterion a
skill can mechanically check.

## Division of labor with existing policy

**Division of labor with existing policy.** This document does NOT restate the
brief-pipeline LaTeX rules or the gate charter — it composes them:

- **L1–L4** (subdomains/brief-system/POLICY.md) own the brief-pipeline
  expression: when gate G6 fires, what satisfies it, and that notes.tex is
  never a no-brainer. On any conflict, L-rules win for brief behavior.
- **he-jwmy** (hecke bead) is the canonical LaTeX-gate charter: the 7 required
  merge artifacts, the covered-file scope definition, and the author-vs-reviewer
  separation.
- **BP-rules** (mathcity/POLICY-beads.md) own bead typing (BP1), research-bead
  protection (BP2/BP5), the bead-side well-formedness of LaTeX beads (BP7), and
  mathematical claim beads (BP8). On conflict, LX wins for LaTeX workflow
  mechanics; BP wins for bead lifecycle.

## Covered file definition

**Covered file**: a .tex file that is named notes.tex at any depth, OR lives
under a latex/notes/ tree, OR is in the depth-2 \input/\include closure of
such a file. Out of scope: files under scratch/tests/fixtures/tmp paths with
no \label{} reachable from the notes-tier reference graph; documented
autogenerated files carrying a % AUTOGENERATED marker.

## Pillar 1 — What LaTeX work is a bead (LX1.x)

The bead system tracks adjudicable LaTeX work. Inline tags track point fixes.
Nothing lives in shadow TODO files.

- LX1.1 Covered .tex changes destined for push/merge are bead-tracked.
  Any diff to a covered file that will be pushed or merged has a bead, and the
  check-latex evidence for that diff is produced with --bead <id> naming
  that bead (evidence lands under ~/gt/tmp-for-taylor/<bead>/).
  Pass: every covered diff presented for adjudication has a named bead and a
  bead-addressed evidence dir. Fail: a covered diff screened as unbeaded, or
  presented with no bead at all → revise (file the bead, re-screen).

- LX1.2 Inline unfinished tags are single-session point markers. The
  unfinished tags check-latex scans for (\taylor, \david, \claude, \note,
  \todo) mark point work inside the document. A tag whose work survives the
  session that created it, or that needs more than one sitting, gets a bead;
  the tag body is then updated to carry the bead ID (e.g.
  \todo{he-xxxx: reconcile with §3.2}). Pass: every unfinished tag older
  than its creating session names a bead. Fail: multi-session work tracked
  only by an anonymous inline tag → revise.

- LX1.3 No shadow TODO surfaces. LaTeX task state never lives in TODO.md,
  a % TODO list comment block, a side todo.tex, or a notebook cell — beads
  are the tracker. Fail: any file or comment block functioning as a LaTeX task
  list → revise (convert entries to beads, delete the list).

- LX1.4 Out-of-scope .tex is unbeaded by default; promotion is a bead.
  Scratch/fixture .tex needs no bead. The moment such a file is promoted into
  the notes tier (moved under latex/notes/, given a reachable \label{}, or
  \input'd within depth 2), the promotion itself is a bead.
  Fail: a promotion diff with no bead → revise.

## Pillar 2 — Stage taxonomy (LX2.x)

Stages are labels on real bd types — never invented types.

- LX2.1 Four stages, expressed as labels:
  - latex-draft — content being written; compile may fail.
  - latex-compilation-ready — compiles clean; labels/refs clean; ready for gate screening.
  - latex-submitted — passed the full gate and shipped to its target.
  - latex-published — target accepted/published; permanent reference.

  The bd type stays in the documented set: task (section-scale work), feature
  (a new paper/deliverable), epic (document-scale migrations), chore (typo
  batches). LaTeX beads additionally carry the [LATEX] domain label (BP7
  convention). Pass: bd show shows a documented type, the [LATEX] label, plus
  exactly one latex-* stage label.

- LX2.2 Stage transitions have mechanical entry criteria.
  - → latex-compilation-ready: check-latex report shows compile.status of pass
    AND check-labels-and-refs verdict PASS.
  - → latex-submitted: full G6 evidence, Taylor's per-diff approval, and the
    submission target recorded in the bead.
  - → latex-published: the permanent identifier (DOI, arXiv version, LMFDB
    knowl ID) recorded in the bead; bead enters research-journal protection
    per BP2.2.

- LX2.3 Stages are monotone per bead. Regression spawns a NEW bead.

- LX2.4 Exactly one stage label at a time.

## Pillar 3 — Quality gates (LX3.x)

check-latex produces evidence, not verdicts.

- LX3.1 check-latex is the mechanical evidence floor: real compile attempt,
  semantic diff summary, evidence block (check-latex-report.{json,md} + tex.diff
  + compile.log) under ~/gt/tmp-for-taylor/<bead>/.

- LX3.2 What check-latex does NOT establish: mathematical correctness, semantic
  equivalence ("losslessness"), or rendered-output identity. A check-latex pass
  is NEVER evidence of any of these.

- LX3.3 Author ≠ reviewer for equivalence claims. Independent compile + PDF-diff
  comes from Mayor or a distinct peer; self-attested losslessness → auto-reject.

- LX3.4 Taylor approves the specific diff, fail-closed. A covered diff may be
  pushed/merged only when latex-approval.toml names Taylor and its
  approved_diff_sha matches the sha under evaluation. Stale or blanket approvals
  do not transfer.

- LX3.5 Review workflow: Taylor compiles, agents never pre-compile for review.
  Source files + README under ~/gt/tmp-for-taylor/<review-name>/; no pdflatex
  run on Taylor's behalf for the review copy.

## Pillar 4 — Atomization (LX4.x)

The unit of LaTeX work is one adjudicable diff — reviewable in one sitting.
The he-d4l monolith (633/97-line restyle) is the canonical failure.

- LX4.1 Default atom: one section of one document.
- LX4.2 Document-scale work is an epic decomposed per section BEFORE work starts.
- LX4.3 Per-claim beads only when a claim gates other work (real bd dep edge).
- LX4.4 Figures and tables ride with their section.
- LX4.5 No per-typo beads; batch cosmetic fixes into one chore per document per pass.
- LX4.6 Adjudicability cap: > ~200 changed lines or > ~3 sections ⇒ split before screening.
- LX4.7 Atomization floor: every intermediate bead in a decomposition must compile and have uniform coverage class.

## Pillar 5 — LaTeX-to-LMFDB coupling (LX5.x)

Mathematical statements that downstream databases cite have two lives.

- LX5.1 Knowl-destined content links an lmfdb bead. Pass: bd show on the LaTeX
  bead lists the lmfdb bead as related/dependent.
- LX5.2 LMFDB labels quoted in prose are verified before close (date + query
  recorded in the bead).
- LX5.3 One direction of truth per statement: either the record generates the
  prose (fix the generator), or the prose is hand-written and the sync bead
  depends on the LaTeX bead.

## Pillar 6 — Merge policy (LX6.x)

Section merges reorder the reference graph — the most label-dangerous edit class.

- LX6.1 merge-latex-sections is ON HOLD (gsp-fby) — do not invoke.
- LX6.2 A merge/reorder is its own bead with pre- and post-label screening.
  Both check-labels-and-refs reports land in the bead's evidence dir.
- LX6.3 Merge beads are pure-move. Content edits discovered mid-merge go to a
  separate bead.

---

## Pillar 7 — Compile-failure MREs (LX7.x)

A compile error that outlives its session gets the check-mre treatment.

- LX7.1 Blocking compile failures get an MRE at scratch/mre-<bead-id>-<slug>.tex
  with header block (ERROR, TOOL, AUTHOR, LAST RUN, STATUS, BEAD).
- LX7.2 MREs stay out of the notes tier by construction.
- LX7.3 MRE polarity flips when the fix lands (STATUS → CONFIRMED FIXED <date>).

---

## Pillar 8 — Computation dependencies (LX8.x)

Numbers in prose are downstream of computations. The dependency is a bd edge.

- LX8.1 Quoted results trace to artifacts (DATA/ path, test-*.mag script, LMFDB label).
- LX8.2 Autogenerated .tex declares itself (% AUTOGENERATED by <generator>) and is
  never hand-edited.
- LX8.3 LaTeX beads block on computation beads via real bd dep edges.
- LX8.4 Recomputes invalidate prose — file the follow-up in the same convoy.
- LX8.5 The notebook loop checkpoints through .mag, not through .tex.

---

## Pillar 9 — Anti-patterns (LX9.x)

Named bead shapes that are rejected on sight.

- LX9.1 The monolith — one bead, whole-document diff.
- LX9.2 No compilation target — bead never names its root .tex file.
- LX9.3 No owner past draft.
- LX9.4 Self-attested losslessness.
- LX9.5 The mixed-concern diff — content + restyle + restructure in one bead.
- LX9.6 Bead-as-journal — using work bead notes as the mathematical record.
- LX9.7 The unbeaded gate dodge.
